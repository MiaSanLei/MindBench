Week Two: Marketing environment & Market Research

Marketing Environment

 Micro

  Stakeholders

   A stakeholder can be any group or individual who can affect or are affected by the achievement of organisational goals.

   3 types

    External stakeholders

     e.g. government, pressure groups, community, professional bodies

    Connected stakeholders

     e.g. shareholders customers, suppliers, bankers

    Internal stakeholders

     e.g. Staff, managers

   Four different reactions to stakeholders

    Proactive

     address problem before it becomes a problem

    Reactive

     Ignore until forced

    Defensive

     block and fight... PR wars, lobbying, courts

    Accommodate

     accept responsibility and tackle issues

   Stakeholder communications

    What are their information needs?

     Information required/needed

     Level of detail and timing

     What do/don't we want them to know?

    What are the best channels?

     Meetings, consultations, questionnaires

     Direct- leaflets, newsletters, exhibitions etc

     Indirect... PR, conferences, sponsorships

 Definition of the environment

  "The forces external to the marketing management function of the firm that impinge on the marketing managements ability to develop and maintain successful transactions with its customers"

Market Research

 Definition

  The objective gathering and recording and analysing of all facts about problems relating to the transfer and sales of goods and services from producer to consumer

 Qualitative Research

  Exploratory research used to uncover consumer motives, attitudes and behaviour

 Quantitative Research

  Research that involves data collection from a sufficient volume of customers to allow for statistical analysis

 Primary Data

  Information collected for a specific purpose or issue to be addressed

 Secondary Data

  Information that already exists somewhere, having been collected for another purpose

 Research Methodology

  Research: Primary or Secondary?

   1. Diagnose the problem (be specific)

   2. Initial investigation

    Secondary data sufficient?

     Yes

     No

      Use primary data research to fill the gaps

   3. Report: conclusions and recommendations

  Types of information

    £ Market growth, % Growth

    Competitor products, prices, advertising spend

    Customer number, location, demographics, segment

   Qualitative Research

    Customer perceptions

    Buying behaviour

    Attitudes and motivations

    Packaging

    Brand image/ preferences

    Reaction to adverts

    External factors

    Power of stakeholders

    DMU's

  Secondary Research

   Internal Desk Research

    Sales and marketing data

    customer databases, EPOS/loyalty

    Production and distribution data

    General market intelligence

    Previous market reports/data

    Free, quick and easy

   External Desk Research

    Govt stats

    Trade/industry reports and media

    Competitors- ads, catalogues, exhibitions

    Directories/journals

    off the shelf reports (mintel, BRAD)

   Problems with secondary research

    Currency

     often out of date

    Accuracy

     may be uncertain/doubtful

    Relevance

     May not quite suit purpose

    Sufficiency

     often only part of the solution

    Availability

     may be limited

  Primary Research

   Experimental/Observation

    Lab tests... tastes, colour etc

    Product trials... in homes

    Product sampling... in store

    Test market... new product, advert etc

    Hidden cameras... in store cctv

    may be time consuming and expensive

   Sampling and surveys

    Statistical sample of population

    may use structured questionnaire

    may use semi structured talk guides

    Face to face, street, interview, doorstep

    Distance...mail, phone, e-mail, fax etc

    Good for lots of quantitative data

   qualitative methods for primary research

    Non statistical methods

    Aim for depth not breadth

    In depth interviews. One to one.

    Focus/discussion groups 7-12 people

    Projective... "my mate"

    Can be used to add depth to quantitative data
