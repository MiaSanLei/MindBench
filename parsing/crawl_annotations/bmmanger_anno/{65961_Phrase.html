Phrases = Group of related words that do not include a subject and verb

Noun Phrase = noun + modifier/s

 The long and winding road

Prepositional Phrase = a preposition + noun or pronoun

 outside the building

Appositive Phrase = renaming or amplifying a word immediately preceeding

 My favourite music, Gregorian chant, is playing.

Particple Phrase = verbals ending in -ing or -ed + complements or modifiers, and acting as an ADJECTIVE.

 Working around the clock, the firefighters put out the fire

Gerund Phrase = verbals that end in -ing and that act as nouns

 Cramming for tests is not a good strategy

Infinitive Phrase = an infinitive + modifiers or complements. Functions as a noun/adjective/adverb

 To subsidize child care is very expensive

Absolute Phrase = Noun or Pronoun + a participle

 Their faces beaming, they collected the prize.
